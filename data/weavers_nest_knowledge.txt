About Weavers Nest
Weavers Nest is a community-based rural tourism initiative supported by NABARD. We work with local tribal communities across Sonitpur, District of Assam to promote responsible tourism, showcase traditional livelihoods, and preserve cultural heritage. Our experiences are designed to connect visitors with the authentic lifestyle, crafts, and natural beauty of the region — all while supporting local families and artisans.

2.	Bird Watching at Nameri National Park and Tiger Reserve
Timing: 6:30 AM – 1:30 PM (Pre-booking required)
Duration: 3 Hours 
Distance: Approx 8 Kilometres 
Booking Period: November – April
Closed: Tuesday (Tuesday birding is closed)
Forest guard cum guide: Mr Minaram Gogoi is one of the best forest guard and bird guide
Other local village cum bird guide: Jitu Darjee, Mintu Ngate, Phukan Mili, Shusil Ngate, Prashanta Das, Bitupan Sut, and Suren Mili. 
What to Experience:
Nameri National Park is home to over 380 bird species, making it a paradise for bird lovers. Some key highlights include:
•	Endangered species: White-winged Wood Duck, Rufous-necked Hornbill, Pallas’s Fish Eagle, White-rumped and Slender-billed Vultures
•	Migratory birds: Ibis bill, Lesser Adjutant, and various shrikes and broadbills
•	Colourful species: Oriental Dwarf Kingfisher, Silver-breasted Broadbill, Long-tailed Broadbill, and Ruddy Kingfisher
Visitors often enjoy the peaceful forest trails along the Jia Bhoroli River — a serene experience surrounded by the sounds of nature.

3.	River Rafting on the Jia Bhoroli River
Timing: 6:30 AM – 1:30 PM (Pre-booking required)
Booking Period: October – May
Time Duration: 2 to 3 hours
Distance: 13 to 15 Kilometres
Experience Level: Level 1 (Beginner-friendly)
Enjoy a gentle and scenic rafting trip perfect for first-timers and families. The 13–15 km route takes around 2 to 3 hours, with calm waters and stunning views of the Nameri National Park on one side and the Tiger Reserve buffer zone on the other.
On clear days, you might see the Eastern Himalayas in the distance. Keep your eyes open for river birds like the Ibis bill, Indian Cormorant, Ruddy Shelduck, River Lapwing, and several Kingfisher species.

Rafting SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)
1. Purpose
To ensure safety, protect the environment, and provide an enjoyable experience for all participants.
2. Raft Capacity
•	Maximum 5 guests per raft
•	2 trained crew members (Head Boatman + Assistant)
•	No exceptions to capacity limits
3. Substance Policy
No alcohol or illegal substances allowed during the trip for safety reasons.
4. Cleanliness Policy
•	Only light snacks and soft drinks in non-breakable containers
•	Carry all waste back — leave no trace
5. Swimming Policy
Swimming is strictly prohibited due to unpredictable river currents and hidden hazards.
6. Life Jacket Policy
Life jackets must be worn and properly fastened at all times.
7. Trip Continuity
Rafts move directly from the start to end point without unscheduled stops.


4.	Jeep Safari at Pakke Tiger Reserve (Arunachal Pradesh)
Location: East Kameng District, Arunachal Pradesh
Activity Type: Guided Jeep Safari
Booking: Pre-booking and permits required
Operating Season: Usually October to April (depends on forest permissions and weather)
Guide: A trained guide will accompany the safari
Inner Line Permit: Required 
Timing: 6:00 AM to 02:00 PM
Time Duration: 3 Hours 
Distance: 12 Km 
Capacity: 5 Tourist per Jeep 

What this experience offers
The jeep safari at Pakke Tiger Reserve gives visitors a close look at the forest ecosystem of Arunachal Pradesh. It is known for its rich wildlife, dense evergreen forests, and peaceful natural setting. This is a great choice for anyone who wants to explore the beauty of the Himalayan foothills by road inside the reserve.
You may come across wildlife such as
•	Asian Elephants
•	Bengal Tigers
•	Clouded Leopards
•	Barking Deer, Sambar, and Goral
•	Hornbills, Eagles, and many forest birds
Pakke is also well known for hornbill conservation efforts, and the forest landscape is ideal for nature photography.

Important Notes
•	The safari route and duration depend on forest authority guidelines
•	Permits and entry fees will apply as per government rules
•	Visitors must follow instructions to avoid disturbing wildlife
